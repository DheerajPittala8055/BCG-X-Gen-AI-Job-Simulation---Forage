Summary:¶

Apple has shown steady financial performance with a 0.30% increase in revenue and a 2.12% rise in net income, indicating strong profitability. While its assets declined by 1.12%, this was accompanied by a 0.57% reduction in liabilities, suggesting controlled financial management. Additionally, Apple’s 1.33% increase in cash flow highlights its operational efficiency, maintaining a healthy financial position despite minor asset reductions.

Microsoft experienced an overall financial decline, with revenue dropping by 6.66% and net income decreasing by 5.79%, reflecting potential market challenges. The 10.33% reduction in assets and 6.40% decline in liabilities indicate a strategic financial shift, possibly involving asset restructuring or debt repayments. However, the 8.15% decline in cash flow raises concerns about liquidity and future investment capabilities, making this a challenging fiscal period for the company.

Tesla’s financial performance presents a mixed picture, with revenue declining by 5.59% and assets dropping by 11.81%, suggesting potential production slowdowns or restructuring. However, net income surged by 31.13%, indicating significant profitability improvements, likely driven by cost optimizations or better margins. Despite an 8.80% reduction in liabilities, Tesla’s cash flow remained relatively stable, showing resilience in managing operational expenses while improving profitability.

Comparing all three companies, Apple stands out as the most stable, with balanced financial growth and controlled liabilities. Microsoft shows the most significant decline, struggling with revenue, net income, and cash flow drops, indicating financial headwinds. Tesla, despite declining revenue, demonstrates strong profitability, suggesting strategic cost management. Overall, Apple and Tesla are in a stronger financial position, while Microsoft faces challenges requiring strategic adjustments.

# Plot revenue growth trends
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 5))
for company in df['Company'].unique():
    company_data = df[df['Company'] == company]
    plt.plot(company_data['Year'], company_data['Revenue Growth (%)'], marker='o', label=company)
plt.xlabel('Fiscal Year')
plt.ylabel('Revenue Growth (%)')
plt.title('Year-over-Year Revenue Growth')
plt.legend()
plt.grid()
plt.show()
